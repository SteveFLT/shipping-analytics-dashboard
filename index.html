Q4 Deep Dive: Cost Spike Diagnostics

Root Cause #1: Carrier Mix Shift

During Q4, severe USPS capacity constraints forced a large portion of volume to shift to UPS — a carrier that commands a significant cost premium for equivalent zones and weights. The capacity limitation, not a service preference, drove the switch.

Root Cause #2: Heavier Orders

Holiday bundling and multi-item orders drove up average package weight. Both of these effects — paying more per shipment for UPS and carrying more weight — compounded simultaneously in Q4.

The Proposal: Dual-Node Fulfillment

The data from the Current Analytics tab establishes a clear mandate: our single-warehouse model in California generates structurally high shipping costs for the majority of our domestic customer base. The following analysis evaluates three real proposals from third-party logistics providers to establish a second fulfillment node on the East Coast or Mid-South.

The Mechanism

Orders are dynamically routed to whichever warehouse produces the lowest shipping cost for that destination ZIP. Only shipments where the 3PL node is genuinely cheaper get redirected — the CA warehouse remains active for the rest.

What's Modeled

Every projection includes the full Total Landed Cost (TLC): shipping freight, pick & pack fees, pallet receiving, and storage — applied to actual 2025 order weights, item counts, and zone data.

How to Read This

Each 3PL section shows the same set of charts: zone improvement, monthly cost comparison vs. baseline, geographic allocation maps, and cost heatmaps. The Executive Modeling Suite at the bottom compares all three side-by-side.
